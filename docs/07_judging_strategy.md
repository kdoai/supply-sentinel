# Judging Strategy

## Main Thesis

Supply Sentinel wins by being practical.

It uses Agentic AI to monitor external risk, but it stays grounded in business operations by connecting those risks to inventory, BOM, orders, and human approval.

## Business Impact

Judging message:

> Raw material supply risk can quickly affect production, delivery, revenue, and customer trust. Supply Sentinel reduces the initial investigation time by automatically identifying affected products, customers, plants, inventory days, and response options.

What to emphasize:

- Procurement impact
- Production continuity
- Customer response speed
- Management visibility
- Reduced dependence on individual know-how

## Approach Effectiveness

Judging message:

> This is not a chat UI. It is a scheduled monitoring agent that collects signals, extracts risk events, checks internal data, decides whether to alert, and prepares response materials.

Agentic behavior:

- Periodic monitoring
- External signal interpretation
- State tracking
- Impact assessment
- Alert decision
- Report generation
- Human escalation

## Completion And Feasibility

Judging message:

> The MVP limits automation to assessment, notification, and recommendation. High-risk business actions remain human-approved, making the solution realistic for enterprise adoption.

What to emphasize:

- Narrow MVP scope
- Sample data can be replaced by real ERP/PLM/CRM data
- Azure Functions is lightweight
- Cosmos DB handles alert history
- Teams fits actual business workflow
- Deterministic calculations improve reliability

## Differentiation

Traditional supply chain AI:

- Demand forecast
- Inventory optimization
- Route optimization
- Production planning
- Recovery after disruption

Supply Sentinel:

- External signal monitoring
- Early warning
- Company-specific impact translation
- First-response automation

## Best One-Liner

> Supply Sentinel translates external supply risks into internal business impact and accelerates first response.

## Best Closing Line

> The value is not predicting the future perfectly. The value is knowing earlier which products, customers, and plants are at risk, and preparing the first response before operations stop.
