# Product Concept

## Concept

Supply Sentinel is a monitoring agent that periodically patrols external and internal information sources, detects early signs of supply disruption, evaluates business impact, and prepares first-response actions.

It should be described as:

> An early-warning agent that turns external supply signals into company-specific impact intelligence.

Avoid describing it as a perfect future-prediction system. The stronger and more credible framing is early warning, impact translation, and first-response acceleration.

## Target Users

- Procurement managers
- Supply chain planning teams
- Production control teams
- Sales operations teams
- Plant managers
- Business continuity managers

## Primary User Pain

When a supply risk appears, teams must manually answer:

- Is this risk relevant to our materials?
- Which products use the affected material?
- Which plants and customers are exposed?
- How many days of inventory remain?
- Are alternatives already approved?
- Who should be notified?
- What should management decide?

This often requires emails, spreadsheets, ERP checks, supplier calls, and manual reports.

## Product Promise

Supply Sentinel reduces the time needed to move from external risk detection to internal response preparation.

## Scope Principle

The product should not try to automate final business decisions.

AI prepares evidence and recommendations. Humans approve important actions.

## Human-In-The-Loop Boundary

AI can automatically:

- Collect information
- Summarize risk
- Extract structured events
- Calculate impact
- Generate alerts
- Draft reports
- Draft response tickets

Humans approve:

- Purchase order changes
- Supplier switching
- Customer-facing announcements
- Price changes
- Major production plan changes
- Contractual decisions

## Differentiation

Traditional supply chain systems often work after internal symptoms appear. They optimize demand plans, inventory, routing, or production schedules.

Supply Sentinel works earlier. It monitors what is happening outside the company and asks whether those events will affect internal operations.

## MVP Design Motto

One scenario, one material, one alert, one convincing business flow.
